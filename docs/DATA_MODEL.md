# Data Model

This document describes the data model for the ecommerce support chatbot. It is meant to explain what the system needs to store, how the main records relate to each other, and which fields matter for product behavior.

This is not the final database schema. It is a product-facing data model that should be understandable to PMs, engineers, scientists, QA analysts, and operators.

## Core Principle

The customer-facing transcript should stay simple.

The customer should see one support conversation with messages from:

- `customer`
- `support`
- `system`

The customer should not need to know whether a support reply was generated by the chatbot, approved by a human operator, edited by a human operator, or written directly by a human operator.

Internally, the system should preserve authorship, confidence, source usage, review decisions, action decisions, and policy context.

```text
Customer-facing transcript is simple.
Internal provenance is detailed.
```

## Entity Relationship Summary

```text
Customer
  has many Conversations

Conversation
  belongs to Customer optionally
  has many Messages
  has many ResponseDrafts
  has many ResponseReviews
  has many Uploads
  has many SupportActions
  has many AgentDecisionTraces

Message
  belongs to Conversation
  may belong to Customer, BotAgent, or OperatorUser through author
  may belong to Customer, BotAgent, or OperatorUser through published_by
  may belong to ResponseDraft
  has many RetrievalResults
  has many Feedback records
  may have one AgentDecisionTrace

BotAgent
  has many ResponseDrafts

ResponseDraft
  belongs to Conversation
  belongs to BotAgent optionally
  may become a published Message
  has many ResponseReviews
  may have one AgentDecisionTrace

ResponseReview
  belongs to Conversation
  belongs to ResponseDraft
  may belong to Message
  may belong to OperatorUser
  may have SupportActions
  may have one AgentDecisionTrace

KnowledgeDocument
  has many RetrievalResults
  has many Messages through RetrievalResults

RetrievalResult
  belongs to Message
  belongs to KnowledgeDocument

Upload
  belongs to Conversation
  may belong to Message

SupportAction
  belongs to Conversation
  may belong to Message
  may belong to ResponseReview

Feedback
  belongs to Message

AgentDecisionTrace
  belongs to Conversation
  belongs to Message
  may belong to BotAgent
  may belong to ResponseDraft
  may belong to ResponseReview

OperatorUser
  has many ResponseReviews

GuardrailPolicy
  has many GuardrailEvaluations

GuardrailEvaluation
  belongs to GuardrailPolicy
  may belong to ResponseDraft
  may belong to Message
```

## Tables

### `customers`

Stores the customer who is asking for support.

Key fields:

- `external_id`: stable identifier from the hypothetical ecommerce business
- `name`: optional display name
- `email`: optional contact email

Relationships:

- A customer can have many conversations.
- A conversation may belong to a customer.

Product notes:

- The first version can support anonymous or demo conversations, so customer can be optional on conversation.
- Later versions may require customer identity to fetch order, payment, return, or account context.
- Customers should be able to view their historical conversations.
- A customer may have many closed conversations over time, but should have at most one active conversation at a time.

### `conversations`

Stores one customer support session.

Key fields:

- `public_id`: customer-facing identifier used in URLs
- `customer_id`: optional link to customer
- `status`: current conversation state
- `category`: optional high-level support category
- `operator_review_requested_at`: when the system requested behind-the-scenes human review
- `resolved_at`: when the issue was resolved or closed

Suggested statuses:

- `open`
- `waiting_on_customer`
- `waiting_on_bot`
- `pending_operator_review`
- `closed`

Relationships:

- Has many messages.
- Has many response drafts.
- Has many response reviews.
- Has many uploads.
- Has many support actions.

Product notes:

- A conversation is the main container for visible customer messages and internal support work.
- `public_id` should be used for customer-facing URLs instead of database IDs.
- The customer may never know whether the conversation is in operator review.
- A customer should have at most one active conversation at a time.
- Active conversations are conversations whose status is not `closed`.
- If a customer starts support while an active conversation already exists, the system should return the existing conversation instead of creating a duplicate.
- Closed conversations should remain available as conversation history.
- Customers should be able to view their own historical conversations.
- Operators should be able to view historical conversations for support review, quality review, and follow-up.
- Historical conversations should preserve their messages, response drafts, response reviews, support actions, uploads, retrieval results, and feedback.

Conversation history behavior:

```text
Customer starts support
  if active conversation exists:
    return existing active conversation
  else:
    create new conversation

Customer views support history
  show open and closed conversations belonging to the customer

Operator views customer support history
  show conversations for the selected customer, including closed conversations
```

### `messages`

Stores published transcript entries visible in the conversation.

A message is different from a draft. If it is in `messages`, it is part of the customer-visible transcript.

Key fields:

- `conversation_id`: parent conversation
- `public_role`: how the message appears in the transcript
- `body`: message text
- `position`: ordering within the conversation
- `origin`: how the message was created internally
- `author_type` / `author_id`: who originally authored it
- `published_by_type` / `published_by_id`: who or what published it
- `response_draft_id`: optional link to the draft that produced it
- `metadata`: structured extra information

Allowed `public_role` values:

- `customer`
- `support`
- `system`

Allowed `origin` values:

- `customer_submitted`
- `bot_auto_sent`
- `bot_approved`
- `bot_edited`
- `operator_replacement`
- `operator_direct`
- `system_event`

Relationships:

- Belongs to a conversation.
- May link to a response draft.
- Has many retrieval results.
- Has many feedback records.

Product notes:

- The customer-facing UI should show `support`, not `bot` or `operator`.
- Internal origin is what allows analytics to separate bot-auto-sent, bot-approved, bot-edited, and operator-written replies.
- `position` should preserve message order.

### `bot_agents`

Stores chatbot configuration.

Key fields:

- `name`: internal bot name
- `provider`: LLM provider, initially OpenAI
- `llm_model`: model name
- `system_prompt`: instruction prompt
- `active`: whether this bot configuration is currently active

Relationships:

- Has many response drafts.

Product notes:

- This table represents the automated chatbot identity and settings.
- It should not be confused with human support operators.

### `response_drafts`

Stores unpublished proposed support replies.

A response draft may become a customer-visible support message, but it does not have to.

Key fields:

- `conversation_id`: parent conversation
- `bot_agent_id`: bot that generated the draft, if generated by bot
- `body`: proposed support response text
- `status`: draft lifecycle state
- `confidence`: model or system confidence score
- `category`: detected support category
- `proposed_action_type`: optional proposed business action
- `proposed_action_payload`: optional action details
- `review_reason`: why review is needed
- `upload_requested`: whether the bot wants the customer to upload something
- `upload_type`: type of upload requested
- `raw_provider_response`: raw LLM/provider output for debugging
- `metadata`: structured extra information

Suggested statuses:

- `draft`
- `pending_review`
- `approved`
- `rejected`
- `published`

Relationships:

- Belongs to a conversation.
- May belong to a bot agent.
- Has many response reviews.
- May produce one published support message.

Product notes:

- Low-confidence drafts should go to operator review.
- High-risk issues should go to operator review even if the bot is confident.
- Drafts are important for measuring bot quality even when the customer never sees them.

### `response_reviews`

Stores human operator review of a response draft.

Key fields:

- `conversation_id`: parent conversation
- `response_draft_id`: draft being reviewed
- `message_id`: optional final published message
- `operator_user_id`: operator performing the review
- `status`: review state
- `reason`: why review was requested
- `summary`: short summary of the case
- `key_decision`: decision needed from the operator
- `decision_payload`: structured decision details
- `edited_body`: operator-edited response body
- `agent_response`: direct operator-written response, if any

Suggested statuses:

- `pending`
- `approved`
- `edited`
- `rejected`
- `resolved`

Relationships:

- Belongs to a conversation.
- Belongs to a response draft.
- May belong to a message.
- May belong to an operator user.
- May have support actions.

Product notes:

- This is the main record for behind-the-scenes human involvement.
- The customer should not see that a response review happened.
- This table supports auditability and future analysis of bot/operator collaboration.

### `agent_decision_traces`

Stores a compact audit record of the chatbot's agent loop for a customer message.

Key fields:

- `conversation_id`: parent conversation
- `message_id`: customer message the bot handled
- `bot_agent_id`: bot configuration used, when available
- `response_draft_id`: generated response draft, when one was created
- `response_review_id`: behind-the-scenes review, when review was required
- `published_message_id`: support message sent to the customer, when auto-published
- `outcome`: final path such as direct answer, upload request, action proposal, human review, or fallback
- `provider_name`: LLM provider or provider abstraction used
- `provider_model`: model configured for the bot
- `response_category`: support category assigned by the bot
- `confidence`: confidence score used for routing
- `review_status`: review state, when review was required
- `review_required`: whether the response was routed to human review
- `retrieved_knowledge_document_ids`: source document ids considered during retrieval
- `proposed_tool_names`: tool/function names the model proposed or used
- `proposed_action_types`: sensitive action types proposed for review
- `metadata`: structured details such as retrieval status, review reason, failure reason, upload type, and customer message text

Suggested outcomes:

- `answered_directly`
- `upload_requested`
- `action_proposed`
- `human_review_requested`
- `fallback`

Relationships:

- Belongs to a conversation.
- Belongs to the customer message being handled.
- May belong to a bot agent.
- May belong to a response draft.
- May belong to a response review.
- May belong to the published support message.

Product notes:

- This is the primary explainability layer for the constrained support agent.
- It helps operators understand why the bot answered, asked for an upload, proposed an action, routed to review, or fell back.
- It supports future evaluation of agent behavior, tool use, confidence routing, retrieval quality, and self-serve efficacy.
- It should not expose hidden human involvement to the customer.

### `operator_users`

Stores authenticated human support operators.

Key fields:

- `email`: operator login email
- `password_digest`: local authentication password digest

Relationships:

- Has many response reviews.

Product notes:

- Initial admin/operator auth can be simple local auth.
- Later versions may add roles, permissions, queues, and assignments.

### `knowledge_documents`

Stores source material the chatbot can use to answer questions.

Key fields:

- `title`: source title
- `source_type`: where the source came from
- `source_identifier`: URL, file ID, reddit thread ID, or other identifier
- `category`: optional topic/category
- `body`: manually entered text
- `extracted_text`: parsed text from upload/scrape/import
- `status`: source processing state
- `created_at`: when the source was added to the system
- `updated_at`: when the source record was last changed
- `source_published_at`: when the original external source was published, if known
- `effective_at`: when the source or policy should start being used
- `expires_at`: when the source or policy should stop being used
- `last_ingested_at`: when the system last fetched or processed the source
- `metadata`: structured extra information

Suggested `source_type` values:

- `manual`
- `upload`
- `url`
- `reddit`

Suggested statuses:

- `draft`
- `processing`
- `active`
- `failed`
- `archived`

Relationships:

- Has many retrieval results.
- Has many messages through retrieval results.

Product notes:

- This supports RAG from the beginning.
- The first retrieval implementation can be keyword-only.
- Source ingestion should stay flexible so future sources like Reddit or URLs can be added.
- The chatbot should prefer active and currently effective sources over expired or outdated sources.
- `created_at` is not always the same as `effective_at`. For example, a policy may be added today but become effective next week.

### `retrieval_results`

Stores which knowledge documents were retrieved for a message or response.

Key fields:

- `message_id`: message associated with retrieval
- `knowledge_document_id`: retrieved source document
- `score`: retrieval relevance score
- `rank`: order of the retrieved result
- `metadata`: structured extra information

Relationships:

- Belongs to a message.
- Belongs to a knowledge document.

Product notes:

- Retrieval results make bot answers explainable and auditable.
- They support analytics on which sources are used most often.
- In a later version, this may move from message-level retrieval to draft-level retrieval if that better matches the bot orchestration flow.

### `uploads`

Stores customer-provided files or images.

Key fields:

- `conversation_id`: parent conversation
- `message_id`: optional message where upload was provided
- `file_type`: type of uploaded content
- `processing_status`: extraction/processing state
- `extracted_text`: parsed text or description from the upload
- `metadata`: structured extra information

Suggested `file_type` values:

- `image`
- `document`
- `other`

Suggested processing statuses:

- `pending`
- `processing`
- `completed`
- `failed`

Relationships:

- Belongs to a conversation.
- May belong to a message.

Product notes:

- Upload should not be an always-on default UX.
- The chatbot may request an upload when needed, such as a photo for a damaged item.
- Image support should eventually feed into the LLM or extraction pipeline.

### `support_actions`

Stores proposed or completed business actions.

Key fields:

- `conversation_id`: parent conversation
- `message_id`: optional related message
- `response_review_id`: optional review that approved or denied the action
- `action_type`: type of business action
- `status`: action state
- `policy_reference_ids`: policy sources used for decision
- `eligibility_reason`: why the action is or is not allowed
- `metadata`: structured extra information

Suggested `action_type` values:

- `refund`
- `return`
- `replacement`
- `credit`
- `cancellation`
- `operator_review`

Suggested statuses:

- `proposed`
- `approved`
- `denied`
- `completed`
- `failed`
- `requires_review`

Relationships:

- Belongs to a conversation.
- May belong to a message.
- May belong to a response review.

Product notes:

- The chatbot can propose actions.
- Some actions may require operator review.
- The actual execution of actions can be stubbed at first and integrated later.

### `feedbacks`

Stores customer feedback on support replies.

Key fields:

- `message_id`: support message being rated
- `rating`: customer rating
- `note`: optional free-text feedback

Suggested ratings:

- `helpful`
- `not_helpful`

Relationships:

- Belongs to a message.

Product notes:

- Feedback should attach to support messages, not customer messages.
- This supports quality measurement and future model improvement.

### `guardrail_policies`

Stores the rules the chatbot and operator workflow should follow.

Guardrails are different from knowledge documents. A knowledge document helps the chatbot answer. A guardrail policy constrains whether an answer is safe, compliant, or allowed to be published.

Key fields:

- `name`: short policy name
- `category`: policy area, such as tone, safety, legal, payment, privacy, refund, or escalation
- `rule_type`: how the rule is enforced
- `description`: plain-language rule description
- `severity`: impact level if the rule is violated
- `status`: lifecycle state
- `effective_at`: when the rule starts applying
- `expires_at`: when the rule stops applying
- `metadata`: structured extra information
- `created_at`: when the guardrail was added
- `updated_at`: when the guardrail was last changed

Suggested statuses:

- `draft`
- `active`
- `archived`

Suggested severity values:

- `low`
- `medium`
- `high`
- `critical`

Example guardrails:

- Use `we`, not `I`.
- Do not reveal whether a human operator is helping behind the scenes.
- Do not promise refunds unless policy and action eligibility allow it.
- Escalate payment-critical, account-critical, legal, privacy-sensitive, fraud, identity, chargeback, or emergency cases.
- Do not ask for image upload unless it is useful for the support decision.

Relationships:

- Has many guardrail evaluations.

Product notes:

- Guardrail policies can be implemented after the MVP, but the data model should leave room for them.
- Some guardrails may be deterministic rules, while others may be LLM-based checks.
- Guardrails should be versioned or timestamped because support policy can change over time.

### `guardrail_evaluations`

Stores the result of checking a response draft or message against guardrail policies.

Key fields:

- `guardrail_policy_id`: rule being evaluated
- `response_draft_id`: draft being checked, if applicable
- `message_id`: published message being checked, if applicable
- `result`: evaluation outcome
- `severity`: impact level of the finding
- `reason`: explanation for the result
- `evaluated_at`: when the check happened
- `metadata`: structured extra information
- `created_at`: when the evaluation record was created
- `updated_at`: when the evaluation record was last changed

Suggested results:

- `passed`
- `warned`
- `failed`
- `requires_review`
- `blocked`

Relationships:

- Belongs to a guardrail policy.
- May belong to a response draft.
- May belong to a message.

Product notes:

- For MVP, guardrail results can live in `ResponseDraft.metadata` or `ResponseReview.reason`.
- Longer term, a separate table makes it easier to audit, debug, and measure guardrail performance.
- A failed or high-severity guardrail evaluation may block publishing or require operator review.

## Example Data Flow

### Customer-visible conversation

```text
Customer: My order arrived damaged.
Support: We can help with that. Please upload a photo of the damaged item.
Customer: [uploads image]
Support: We have processed a refund.
```

### Internal records

```text
Conversation
  public_id: conv_123
  status: pending_operator_review

Message
  public_role: customer
  origin: customer_submitted
  author: Customer
  body: My order arrived damaged.
  position: 1

ResponseDraft
  bot_agent: active OpenAI bot
  body: We can help with that. Please upload a photo of the damaged item.
  confidence: 82%
  upload_requested: true
  upload_type: image
  status: published

Message
  public_role: support
  origin: bot_auto_sent
  response_draft: ResponseDraft
  body: We can help with that. Please upload a photo of the damaged item.
  position: 2

Upload
  file_type: image
  processing_status: completed

ResponseDraft
  body: We should refund this order.
  confidence: 62%
  proposed_action_type: refund
  review_reason: Refund decision below confidence threshold.
  status: pending_review

ResponseReview
  operator_user: OperatorUser
  key_decision: refund_eligibility
  status: approved
  edited_body: We have processed a refund.

SupportAction
  action_type: refund
  status: completed
  eligibility_reason: Damaged item confirmed by uploaded image.

Message
  public_role: support
  origin: bot_edited
  published_by: OperatorUser
  response_draft: ResponseDraft
  body: We have processed a refund.
  position: 4
```

## Metrics Supported By The Data Model

This data model should support:

- Self-serve efficacy
- Operator review rate
- Bot confidence distribution
- Draft approval rate
- Draft edit rate
- Draft rejection rate
- Policy-compliant resolution rate
- Customer feedback by support reply
- Knowledge source usage
- Support action approval and completion rates
- Agent decision trace outcomes
- Tool/action proposal frequency
- Repeat contact rate across historical conversations
- Active conversation age
- Historical conversation reopen or follow-up rate
- Guardrail pass, warning, failure, and block rates
- Most frequently triggered guardrail policies

Self-serve efficacy must not be optimized by hiding, delaying, or discouraging legitimate operator review. A self-serve session counts as successful only when the issue is plausibly resolved, policy-compliant, and not merely deflected.

## Open Questions

- Should retrieval results attach to `messages`, `response_drafts`, or both?
- Should support actions be created before review as proposed actions, or only after review approval?
- Should `Conversation.status` expose `pending_operator_review`, or should operator review state live only on `response_reviews`?
- Should `Upload` store image understanding output separately from extracted text?
- Should policy compliance decisions be stored on `response_drafts`, `response_reviews`, or `support_actions`?
- Should customers be allowed to reopen a closed conversation, or should follow-up always create a new conversation?
- How long should conversation history remain visible to customers and operators?
- Should anonymous/demo conversations support history through browser session, or only authenticated customers?
- Should guardrail policies be managed in the admin product, seed data, or configuration files?
- Should guardrail evaluations attach to `response_drafts`, `messages`, or both?
- Which guardrails should block publishing automatically versus route to operator review?
